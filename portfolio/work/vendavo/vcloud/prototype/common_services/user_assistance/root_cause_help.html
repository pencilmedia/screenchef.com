User Assistance: Root Cause Comparison

What is plotted on this chart?

The Root Cause Comparison Chart presents transaction price adjusters as a positive or negative percentage of the Market Price. It allows you to compare price adjustments for transactions identified as poor performing versus those that have performed well. By comparing the price adjustments for good transactions to bad transactions, you can quickly see which adjustments created the performance discrepancy, such as your bad transactions having much higher discounts and service cost than your good transactions.

Why do some price adjustments have such a huge difference in their colored bars?

The analysis identifies the adjustment with the largest difference in the percentage of market price; that adjustment is the opportunity root cause. The opportunity value is calculated based on bringing the root cause adjustment to the defined floor.

How can I drill into more details?

When you hover over a specific bar in the chart, you see the breakdown of how the transactions performed for a particular price adjustment in relation to Market Price. Use the product selector to the right to see the performance of transactions for a particular product; filtering for a specific product shows you the floor for the price adjustment identified as the root cause. The floor is generated by Vendavo Profit Advisor based on our industry knowledge and experience.

What can I do to improve the red transactions?

Review the content available at the top of the Opportunity Detail page for recommended actions to resolve the root cause of the profit leak. By visualizing the differences for each price adjustment, you determine if creating or changing a policy can improve the profitability of your bad transactions.

Why don't I see the orange and dark blue bars?
